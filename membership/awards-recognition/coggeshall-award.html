Coggeshall Award

This award was established in 1956 as a result of a bequest to the Association by Allan Coggeshall to honor a member of the Association who is adjudged to have made an outstanding contribution to the progress of the industry in the Technical and Training Service field.

  1. Home
  2. Membership
  3. Awards & Recognition
  4. Coggeshall Award

In his will, Allan Coggeshall, a member of the firm Hatzel & Buehler, Inc. in New York City, bequeathed a sum of $2,500 to the National Electrical Contractors Association. In 1956, the NECA Executive Committee established The Coggeshall Award.

This Award is given annually the NECA member adjudged to have made an outstanding contribution to the industry in technical or training activities. To qualify for this Award, the member shall have contributed an idea or performed a service which has proved to be particularly beneficial in the technical or training field.

Nominations are accepted no later than May 1, and the Award is announced after the Executive Committee's decision at their June meeting.
